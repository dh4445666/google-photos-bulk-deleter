Google Photos Bulk Deleter

Install this Chrome Extension once, then delete all your Google Photos with a single click — no coding, no console, ever.

🔧 One-time setup (takes ~1 minute)
1

Unzip the downloaded folder

You should have downloaded a file called google-photos-bulk-deleter.zip. Right-click it → Extract All (Windows) or double-click (Mac). You'll get a folder.

2

Open Chrome Extensions page

In Chrome, type this in your address bar and press Enter:

🔒 chrome://extensions
3

Turn on "Developer mode"

Look for the Developer mode toggle in the top-right corner of the page and turn it ON.

Developer mode
ON
4

Click "Load unpacked" and select the folder

A button called Load unpacked will appear at the top-left. Click it, then browse to and select the gpdt-extension folder you unzipped in Step 1.

📂 Select the gpdt-extension folder
5

Done! The extension is installed ✅

You'll see "Google Photos Bulk Deleter" appear in your extensions list. You only need to do this setup once.

🎉 From now on — it's just one click

Every time you want to delete photos, just go to photos.google.com and click the extension icon 🗑️ in your Chrome toolbar.

⬇️
Extension popup appears
▶ Start Deleting Photos
Click and the tool launches instantly
⚠️ Photos are moved to Trash, not immediately deleted permanently. Google keeps them for 60 days before erasing forever — so you have time to recover mistakes.
This extension runs entirely in your browser. No data is sent anywhere.
